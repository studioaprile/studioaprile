Material Recording

Material Recording is a method for producing, observing and describing how wood behaves under constraint, intervention and time. It treats the object as a changing condition rather than a finished form.

Process

Each object begins with a defined geometry, a specific timber and a set of conditions. Pressure, exposure and selected interventions are introduced in a controlled way so that response can be observed rather than predicted.

Failure

Cracking, rupture, distortion and collapse are not rejected as defects. They are the moments that make the material legible, showing where resistance ends and behaviour becomes visible.

State

Objects are recorded across time as active, stable or resolved. The work is therefore not fixed at a single outcome; it is structured around the transitions an object passes through.

Material

Wood is approached as a variable material with memory, stress and direction. Grain, moisture, density and cut are not background properties but the conditions that shape each record.

Position

Material Recording sits between sculpture, research and documentation. Its position is observational rather than expressive: to construct situations in which the material can register its own behaviour with precision.
